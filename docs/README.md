# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the centralized hub for OctoAcme's project management processes, templates, and knowledge.

## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme applies a structured yet adaptable approach to project management, emphasizing collaboration, clarity, and continuous improvement. Projects move through a lifecycle of initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective review. The process begins with a one-pager summarizing the problem, goals, success metrics, stakeholders, timelines, and risks, ensuring early alignment and clarity on business value and scope. Once initiated, teams engage in structured planning with kickoffs, breaking work into actionable items with acceptance criteria, estimating effort, and identifying dependencies and the Definition of Done.

Key roles are well-defined: the Project Manager (PM) coordinates delivery and communication; Product Managers define and prioritize outcomes; Developers build, test, and deliver solutions in line with requirements and quality standards; QA/Testers validate features; and Stakeholders provide inputs and approvals. Regular standups, syncs, and stakeholder updates are core to OctoAcme’s communication cadence, with risks and dependencies actively tracked and reviewed.

Communication follows regular routines—team standups, cross-functional syncs, stakeholder updates, and escalation paths for timely risk management. A single source of truth—typically the project README or a release doc—keeps all parties up to date.

Quality assurance is embedded throughout: small, peer-reviewed pull requests anchored to issues with clear acceptance criteria; automated testing and security scanning before merge; manual QA for critical scenarios; and release checkpoints for completion and rollback planning. Retrospectives after milestones capture learnings and fuel continuous improvement, underpinning reliable, valuable delivery at scale.
